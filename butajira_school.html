Butajira elementary school


The fundamental goal of instituting higher standards of care for patients everywhere is secured by infrastructure as a major pillar. Health infrastructure must augment the seven quality domains of healthcare â€” patient experience, effectiveness, efficiency, timeliness, safety, equity, and sustainability.

Butajira is a town situated in the Gurage Zone of the Southern Nations, Nationalities and Peoples' Region (SNNPR) of Ethiopia at the foot of the Zebidar Massif. Butajira and several woredas in its vicinity are served by the Butajira General Hospital as a vital referral center. More than 100,000 patients are treated annually at this hospital. It performs from 5500 to 6000 deliveries a year, of which 10-15% are Caesarean sections. Currently, 60 to 70 new patients visit the Emergency Department on a daily basis.

Though the hospital serves a large catchment, it is short on the infrastructure and necessary resources to handle the demand. In an effort to ameliorate this infrastructural lacuna, SHS is undertaking a turn-key project scheduled to deliver in 6 months' time. This project integrates all essential components of infrastructure including auxiliary components like equipment, HR, information technology (IT), systems, and processes.

2 months in, we are on schedule and effecting the contract terms in a parallel timeline.
